Compliance for Exports from India to the US

1. Export Policy and Licensing

Importer Exporter Code (IEC): Mandatory for all exports; issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT).

Trade Regulations:

  • Adhere to the U.S. International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) and Export Administration Regulations (EAR) for restricted or controlled goods.
  • Ensure compliance with Anti-Dumping Duty (ADD) and Countervailing Duty (CVD) laws to avoid trade violations.

2. Documentation

Mandatory Documents:

  • Commercial Invoice: Includes product details, value, and trade terms.
  • Bill of Lading: Proof of goods handed over to the carrier for shipment.
  • Packing List: Detailed breakdown of goods, including weights and dimensions.
  • Certificate of Origin (CoO): Required to verify the origin of the goods.
  • FDA Approval: Necessary for food, drugs, cosmetics, and related products.

Additional Filing:

  • File Electronic Export Information (EEI) through the Automated Export System (AES) for shipments valued over $2,500.

3. Tariffs and Regulatory Compliance

Harmonized Tariff Schedule of the US (HTSUS):

  • Classify goods under the HTS to determine applicable tariffs and duties.

Customs Standards:

  • Align with the requirements of the U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) for smooth processing and risk mitigation.

4. Export Incentives

Duty Drawback Scheme:

  • Reclaim duties paid on imported goods that are later exported.

Generalized System of Preferences (GSP):

  • Check for eligibility to export goods duty-free to the US under this scheme.

5. Customs and Risk Management

Pre-Clearance:

  • Use AES for customs clearance to avoid delays.

Risk Mitigation:

  • Screen buyers against the Denied Persons List (DPL) and Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List to ensure compliance with sanctions.

Insurance:

  • Obtain marine insurance to cover risks during transit.
